Bottom line

The :15/:45 slot configuration had a real but limited operational impact on individual agents — most significantly Kyara and Ace — accounting for an estimated 2.5% reduction in potential meetings during the affected window. Critically, the rate at which clients who were shown a calendar went on to book a meeting did not decline during this period — ruling out a negative client-side effect. The broader decline in total meetings between Sept/Oct 2025 and Jan/Feb 2026 is driven primarily by a 28% drop in qualified lead volume, not the slot configuration.

01

The slot change did create a real operational problem — for some agents

When a client booked a :15 or :45 slot, the resulting gap before and after the meeting could prevent another booking from fitting in the same hour. This is the issue the team raised, and the data confirms it happened.

Scale of the effect

9.1% of all bookings landed on a :15 or :45 slot. Of all agent-hours worked during the window, 6.9% were blocked to a single meeting due to a :15/:45 booking. Estimated meetings lost: ~134, or 2.5% of total meetings in the period.

Most affected agents

Impact was unevenly distributed. Kyara Canedo had 19% of her bookings land on :15/:45 slots (43 blocked hours). Ace Barcelona had 14% (36 blocked hours). Most other agents were below 13%.

02

No evidence of client-side harm — booking rate held steady throughout

The Chili Piper Form Conversion data tracks every client who was shown a booking calendar and whether they completed a booking (close rate = meetings scheduled ÷ calendar displayed). If the :15/:45 slots created confusion or friction, we would expect this rate to fall during the affected period. It did not.

Key finding

The close rate was stable across all configuration states — 87–88% before the change, 91% during the :15/:45 window, 93% after the revert. No configuration produced a decline. The data contains no evidence that slot configuration, in either direction, materially affected whether clients completed a booking.

03

The headline meetings decline is explained by fewer qualified leads entering the funnel

Sept/Oct 2025 and Jan/Feb 2026 are structurally different periods. Lead volume, campaign performance, and agent headcount all shifted between them. These factors — not calendar configuration — account for the difference in meeting totals.

  • ↓
    Qualified lead volume fell 28% Sept/Oct 2025 averaged 2,375 qualified leads per month at 105/day. Jan/Feb 2026 averaged 1,701 per month at 81/day. Ad spend was comparable (~AUD 450k vs ~AUD 424k), but efficiency dropped from 55 leads per AUD 10k to 38–42 — meaning the same budget generated fewer qualified prospects.
  • ↑
    Agent headcount grew from 10 to 12–14 during the same window Three new agents joined between January and February 2026 (Gareth, Lisa, Jorge), with two more added in April. When headcount grows faster than lead volume, meetings per agent per day falls arithmetically — even if total throughput is stable. This is a ramp-up effect, not a capacity problem.
  • →
    Cancellation rate was unchanged across both periods Sept/Oct 2025: 8.9%. Jan/Feb 2026: 8.4–8.7%. No step-change at the slot configuration date. The slot change did not affect whether booked meetings were kept.
  • ↓
    November 2025 suppression preceded the config change The decline in meetings per agent began in November 2025 — two months before the slot change. This was driven by a Google Ads reset that reduced qualified lead volume from 97/day to 70/day. The December ads pause compounded this. The slot change was introduced into an already-weakened pipeline.
Findings & Close-out
:15/:45 Slots — Closed

Reverting to :00/:30 slots was the right call to remove calendar friction for the sales team, with some agents more affected than others during the window. However, this change will not materially affect total meeting volume — the data found no evidence that the slot configuration impacted overall bookings. The issue was operational and agent-side; it has been resolved.

Lead Volume — In Discussion

The funnel from calendar displayed to meeting booked is performing well, with conversion rates at their strongest point in the analysis period. However, qualified lead volume remains below October 2025 levels while team headcount has grown. Understanding any changes to Ads targeting, ICP definition, or geographic focus would help identify how to return to higher lead volumes.

Internal action in progress: ICP analysis review underway based on existing client base.
